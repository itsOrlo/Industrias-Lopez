Embark on a Journey of Discovery in the Amazon Rainforest with Cielo Verde

The Amazon rainforest, often dubbed the "lungs of the Earth," is a natural wonder that captivates the imagination of all who venture into its depths. At Cielo Verde, we invite you to explore this unparalleled ecological masterpiece through our immersive wildlife tours. As a company deeply committed to environmental conservation and responsible tourism, we provide an experience that not only educates but also leaves a positive impact on the pristine landscapes we are fortunate to visit.

The Amazon: A Biodiversity Hotspot Like No Other

The Amazon rainforest is, without a doubt, the largest tropical rainforest on the planet, and its sheer scale is mind-boggling. The forest buzzes, hums, chirps, and bubbles with life in a way that is unmatched anywhere else on Earth. To put it into perspective, a single Amazonian tree can be home to more species of ants than all the British Isles combined. Furthermore, one hectare of this lush forest hosts as many species of frogs as you'll find in all of North America. The sheer vastness of the jungle harbors more than twenty percent of the world's vascular plant species. Here, you can encounter a monkey small enough to rest on the tip of your finger, an eight-pound toad, a bird-eating spider, and even the world's largest snake, the awe-inspiring 30-foot anaconda.

Discover a Birdwatcher's Paradise with Cielo Verde: Unveiling Nature's Avian Treasures

For birdwatching enthusiasts, the Amazon rainforest in Ecuador is a true paradise. With an astonishing diversity of over 587 bird species, it offers an unparalleled opportunity to observe these feathered wonders in their natural habitat. At Cielo Verde, we take great pride in our birdwatching trips, which are a top attraction for visitors to the region. With our expert guides by your side, you can expect to spot approximately 250 to 300 bird species during your adventure. From colorful macaws to elusive toucans, the Amazon's avian inhabitants will leave you in awe.

In Ecuador's part of the Amazon, Yasuní National Park stands out as a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and conservationists alike. The biodiversity found here is staggering, even by Amazonian standards. Yasuní boasts an impressive 150 amphibian species, surpassing the combined totals of the United States and Canada. It is also home to 121 reptile species, over 200 mammal species, a remarkable 596 bird species, and a diverse array of 382 fish species. The richness of life in Yasuní is a testament to the importance of preserving this precious ecosystem.

Explore the Enchanted World of the Galápagos Islands with Cielo Verde

The Galápagos Islands, a province of Ecuador, are situated approximately 1,000 kilometers off the coast. This remote location has nurtured an incredible diversity of plant and animal species, many of which are found nowhere else on the planet. The Galápagos' isolated terrain has become a livinglaboratory for the study of evolution, and it all began with one man's voyage.

Darwin's Legacy

In 1835, Charles Darwin set foot on the Galápagos Islands, forever altering the course of scientific thought. His observations of the distinct species inhabiting these islands played a pivotal role in the development of his theory of evolution by natural selection. Today, the Galápagos Islands, along with the surrounding waters, constitute the Galápagos Province of Ecuador, the Galápagos National Park, and the Galápagos Marine Reserve.

Recommended Sites

During your visit with Cielo Verde, you'll have the chance to explore some of the most captivating sites in the Galápagos Islands:

Tortuga Bay

Encounter marine iguanas, white-tip reef sharks, and the famous Galápagos tortoises at this stunning beach.

Giant Tortoise Sanctuary

Visit the Giant Tortoise Sanctuary and witness these magnificent creatures up close in their natural habitat.

Lava Tunnels Exploration

Explore the mesmerizing lava tunnels and snorkel in the crystal-clear waters around Kicker Rock, a volcanic formation teeming with a diverse range of marine life.

Charles Darwin Research Station

Learn about ongoing conservation efforts and observe giant tortoises up close.
